The Mana Curve Strategic Philosophy Quiz

A personality test based on Magic: The Gathering, the hit collectible trading card game. Yes, really.

Magic the Gathering has 5 colours, each of which have quite distinct personalities. They combine together in ways which create more complex types. In one Magic format, you pick a "Commander", a legendary creature which has a combination of these types. The colours of that commander define what kinds of cards you can play, and the various colour combinations tend to have distinct play-styles. We suggest that peoples' preferred play-style reflects something about who they are AND that the 5 colours, 31 combinations actually make quite a useful diagnostic/descriptive personality framework. At worst it's a bit of fun, innit?

› Learn more about why this exists

Many personality frameworks tend to use colours. DISC uses four. True Colors uses four. The colours tend to be a bit easier to remember than letter codes and give us a shared language to say "Ohhhh, Bruce isn't a jerk, he's just blue" (incorrect, by the way, Bruce IS a jerk AND is blue, they're not mutually inconsistent)

If you've taken more than one of these, you'll know that the colour choice, the choice of behaviours and all that tend to be... fine... but it's not exactly science, right? The test makers and markers will say until they're blue (or red) in the face that it's just a descriptive tool. So... why not do the same thing with the mana colours?

So we asked whether a personality framework based on Magic: The Gathering — five colours. We created/synthesised fully realised philosophies behind each using the thirty years of lore and we realised it sorta works.

Magic players probably already know this. They've got colours that they enjoy playing, and colours that they don't. They judge people that play certain colours. There are stereotypes, and interactions and banter. It all sounds awfully like a shared language for describing personality!

So our thesis is this -- We think that M:tG is applicable as a workplace psychology framework. So we turned it into one.

How This Works
I
Enemy Pairs · 30 questions
Thirty forced choices between opposing philosophies/colours. Through this process we will, regrettably, learn things about you.
II
Refinement · 6 – 12 questions
The second round is harder, and tests pairs of colours which are closer to each other, to help us discriminate between similar types.
III
Final Determination · 3 – 6 questions
In step III we have a few candidate options, and we put them Head-to-head until all but one wizard admits defeat.

            Your Colors

            Communication Strategies

            How to effectively communicate with each color

            Talking to WHITE

            ✓ DO
            • • Frame in terms of team benefit
            • • Acknowledge fairness concerns
            • • Show proper process
            • • Build consensus
            ✗ DON'T
            • • Present as purely self-serving
            • • Skip process or ignore rules
            • • Make unilateral decisions
            • • Rush without buy-in

            Talking to BLUE

            ✓ DO
            • • Provide data and analysis
            • • Explain the logic
            • • Give time to think
            • • Respect expertise
            ✗ DON'T
            • • Rush without analysis
            • • Appeal only to emotion
            • • Make unsubstantiated claims
            • • Be imprecise or vague

            Talking to BLACK

            ✓ DO
            • • Be direct about mutual benefit
            • • Respect boundaries
            • • Negotiate clearly
            • • Don't waste time
            ✗ DON'T
            • • Expect altruism
            • • Be indirect or hint
            • • Appeal only to morality
            • • Assume they'll help for free

            Talking to RED

            ✓ DO
            • • Be authentic and passionate
            • • Get to the point quickly
            • • Bring energy
            • • Act, don't just plan
            ✗ DON'T
            • • Over-analyse everything
            • • Be cold or emotionless
            • • Make them wait unnecessarily
            • • Constrain their freedom

            Talking to GREEN

            ✓ DO
            • • Give them time
            • • Respect precedent
            • • Don't force immediate change
            • • Value their experience
            ✗ DON'T
            • • Rush or pressure
            • • Dismiss tradition
            • • Force artificial solutions
            • • Ignore accumulated wisdom

            Conflict Resolution

            Understanding and resolving the five core color tensions

            ↔

            Community vs. Self-Interest

            Source: White prioritizes team, Black prioritizes self

            Resolution: Clarify when each value applies ("our people first" = Black's tribe, broader community = White's fairness)

            ↔

            Planning vs. Action

            Source: Blue wants more analysis, Red wants to act now

            Resolution: Time-box planning phase, then execute with learning loops

            ↔

            Extraction vs. Sustainability

            Source: Black maximises short-term, Green preserves long-term

            Resolution: Define regeneration rate, extract within sustainable limits

            ↔

            Freedom vs. Order

            Source: Red wants autonomy, White wants coordination

            Resolution: Minimum viable structure with clear boundaries but freedom inside

            ↔

            Acceptance vs. Improvement

            Source: Green accepts what works, Blue wants to optimise everything

            Resolution: Preserve what demonstrably works, improve what demonstrably doesn't

            De-escalation Strategies

            When someone is upset, here's what they need

            When WHITE is upset:

            Acknowledge impact on team, show concern for fairness, propose systematic solution, rebuild community connection

            When BLUE is upset:

            Provide analysis and data, show logical reasoning, give time to process, address inefficiency directly

            When BLACK is upset:

            Acknowledge their interests weren't served, negotiate clear resolution, make explicit commitments, respect their boundaries

            When RED is upset:

            Let them express emotion, don't invalidate feelings, act quickly to resolve, bring energy and passion

            When GREEN is upset:

            Give them time and space, don't force immediate change, respect their experience, slow down the pace
